## What changed since Round 1

You told us last round that about 40 percent of the leads were not real targets. Examples were Timac Agro and Valagro (agrochemicals, wrong supply chain), Lush and Dr. Barbara Sturm (only sell their own brand), Sharon Personal Care (ingredient supplier, not finished goods), and N.R. Spuntech (your direct competitor in wipes).

We turned your feedback into a playbook. Every new company is now read through that playbook by a senior-BDR layer that asks the questions you would ask:

1. Are they in the right supply chain (physical inputs vs chemistry)?
2. Do they actually buy from third parties, or do they only sell their own brand?
3. Is this a producer who could resell complementary Capricorn SKUs, or a pure brand house with no reseller motion?
4. Are they on the named-competitor list?

For each company that passes, we also pre-fill the answer to "what could Capricorn sell them" using your real catalog (cups, cutlery, packaging, pet food, geotextiles, films, nets, hoses, irrigation, cosmetics, wipes, and so on). So you no longer need to think about that field, it is filled in for every lead.

## How the system works now

Four steps, same as before, with two new steps in the middle.

1. **Find.** We search for companies by industry, by website language, by size, by revenue, and by country. The agriculture keywords were rewritten so we now look for the actual products you sell (agrotextiles, mulch film, anti-hail nets, drip irrigation, grow bags) instead of generic "agriculture", which last round pulled in agrochemical companies.
2. **Drop the obvious wrong ones early.** A keyword blacklist drops agrochemicals, biostimulants, pesticides, fragrance, cosmetic ingredients, contract manufacturers, animal feed, and construction chemicals before we spend any credits or AI calls on them. This round, that gate dropped 9 companies on its own.
3. **Score with fixed rules.** Same 100-point model as before: industry 35, staff 10, revenue 15, website keywords 20, country 20.
4. **BDR judgment layer (new).** A Claude model trained on your Round 1 feedback reads each company and applies your playbook. It can downgrade or reject companies the deterministic score would have kept. This round it dropped 32 of the 52 companies we enriched, mostly for the reasons you cited in Round 1 (ingredient suppliers, construction chemicals, medical-clinical, direct competitor, DTC-only, native-ingredient-only).
5. **Pre-fill what to sell.** The same BDR layer writes a specific SKU list per company, so you do not have to.
6. **Find the contact.** Same as Round 1.

## Costs and budgets

This round used about 109 Explorium credits (52 for company enrichment, 57 for the 21 contact enrichments across two passes) plus 28 cents of Anthropic API for the BDR judgment layer. Total under one dollar plus the credits, vs ~143 credits in Round 1.

The contact-finding step was tuned mid-round after we noticed too many empty rows. Our initial filter (procurement department + verified email) was too narrow for European SMEs, who often don't tag a procurement department. Dropping that filter and adding a country gate surfaced the real buyers (SCHOELLKOPF Managing Director, Ecocraft Commercial Director, Armos Operations Manager, plus three sales managers we missed the first pass). The workflow is now patched for future rounds.
