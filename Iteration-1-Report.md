25 of the 27 companies are Tier 1. Tier 1 is the best-fit group.

## 2. How the system works

The system works in four steps.

**Step 1, Find.** It looks for companies by their industry, by the words on their website, by staff size, by revenue, and by country.

**Step 2, Check.** It pulls real facts about each company, such as staff count, revenue, and what the company makes.

**Step 3, Score.** It scores each company against your customer profile with fixed rules and no guessing. First it drops companies that do not fit, such as ones that are the wrong size, in the wrong country, shops, agencies, machine makers, and firms that resell other brands. Then it gives each company a score out of 100. Industry fit is worth 35 points, staff size 10, revenue 15, website keywords 20, and country 20. A score of 70 or higher is Tier 1. A score of 45 to 69 is Tier 2.

**Step 4, Contact.** It finds the right person to reach at each company, with a checked email address and a LinkedIn page.

## 4. Coverage and data quality

By product area: Cosmetics 12, Foodservice Disposables 4, Agriculture 4, Membranes and Geotextiles 3, Pet Food 2, Wipes 2. Tiers: 25 companies are Tier 1, and 2 are Tier 2.

Two checks, warehouse and own-brand-only, are marked for a quick human review on most leads. Company websites rarely state these things clearly, so the system flags them for a short check instead of guessing. A few were confirmed on their own.

Four companies have no contact in the data source: Lush España, MONGE, Timac Agro, and SCHOMBURG. They are listed as company records, so you can find a contact by hand.

Emails are checked where possible. A few are "catch-all". This means the company domain accepts mail, but the exact mailbox cannot be confirmed.
